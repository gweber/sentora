# Commercial License

Sentora is open-source software licensed under the [GNU AGPL-3.0](LICENSE). The AGPL requires that any modified version of Sentora made available over a network must also be released under the AGPL, including the source code of your modifications.

## When You Need a Commercial License

A commercial license is required if you want to:

- **Deploy a modified version** of Sentora as a hosted/SaaS product without releasing your source code.
- **Embed or integrate** Sentora into proprietary software.
- **Redistribute** Sentora as part of a commercial product (OEM).
- **Remove the AGPL obligations** for internal enterprise use where AGPL compliance is not feasible.

## When You Do NOT Need a Commercial License

You do **not** need a commercial license if you:

- Use the unmodified software internally (the AGPL permits this).
- Use the software and comply with all AGPL terms (including making modified source available).
- Contribute to the open-source project.

## Pricing

Commercial licenses are negotiated on a per-organisation basis. Typical models include:

- **Single-organisation license** — perpetual or annual, for internal use.
- **OEM / embedding license** — for redistribution within a commercial product.
- **SaaS license** — for offering Sentora functionality as part of a hosted service.
